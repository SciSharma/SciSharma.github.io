Phenotypic Clustering of Rare Diseases: Ciliopathies

Active · PhD

This doctoral research investigates shared molecular mechanisms underlying phenotypically clustered rare diseases, with a primary focus on ciliopathies. The work integrates Ayurveda-based phenotype annotation with an expectation–maximization (EM) algorithm, revealing six biological clusters with implications for resolving ciliopathies. Multi-omic and genomic datasets are analyzed using network-based and computational approaches to identify convergent disease signatures.

TAS2R38 Polymorphism & Bitter Taste Perception

Collaborative

Contributed to a collaborative study investigating genetic variation in bitter taste perception via the TAS2R38 receptor. Performed machine learning-based clustering and integrative analysis of IMPPAT and BitterDB datasets to identify patterns linking phytochemical profiles with receptor responses and phenotypic variability.
