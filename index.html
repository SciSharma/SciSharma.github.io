PhD Research Focus

Common molecular roots of rare ciliopathies

biotech

My research aims to bridge the gap between genotype and phenotype by identifying molecular hubs that influence complex biological systems. I focus on understanding how genetic variation—both canonical and non-canonical—impacts ciliary function and rare disease pathways using integrative multi-omics and computational approaches.

By leveraging network-based and computational methods, integrating Ayurvedic phenotypic annotation with modern omics, and applying machine learning to multi-omic datasets, I aim to identify convergent disease signatures and shared molecular mechanisms across rare diseases.
